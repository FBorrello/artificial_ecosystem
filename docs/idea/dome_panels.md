# Dome Panels Detailed Specifications

This document provides detailed specifications for the 465 panels in the dome: 352 transparent polycarbonate panels and 113 wooden panels. 58 gores for layers 1-8, last layer one wooden round cap panel. Panels are staggered by 1/3 panel width between layers for enhanced structural integrity.

## Polycarbonate Panels Specifications
- **Parts**: Two polycarbonate sheets (internal and external walls), bent borders, bolts, silicon seals.
- **Materials**: Polycarbonate sheets 4 mm thick, bolts, silicon sealant.
- **Assembly and Manufacturing**: Each panel requires 2 full polycarbonate sheets (bulk format 2050x3050 mm, 4 mm thick). Sheets are dried for 24 hours before thermoforming. Internal panel is thermoformed first using a mold that creates the connector for pressurization, then cut to final size. External panel is thermoformed on a composite mold consisting of: sphere section (external mold), the internal panel, and internal mold (hard and soft parts). Thermoforming uses pressurization between panels and vacuum on the internal mold. After thermoforming, the internal panel remains trapped; remove hard part of internal mold, then soft part, then cut to final size. Transparent silicon is applied on the internal panel before mating. The internal mold creates negative curvature on the external panel to prevent condensation and bolt housing pockets via vacuum holes. Panels staggered by 1/3 panel width between layers.
- **Specifications**: Pressurized to 0.5 bar for structural strength, waterproof connections, staggered for load distribution.

## Wooden Panels Specifications
- **Parts**: Wooden core panel, white PVC 1 mm UV-stabilized sheets for interior and exterior surfaces.
- **Materials**: Wood, PVC sheets.
- **Assembly and Manufacturing**: Shape wooden core, wrap with PVC sheets on both sides, secure.
- **Specifications**: Non-transparent, UV-stabilized, durable.

## Panel Assembly Process
1. Thermoform polycarbonate sheets to curved trapezoid shapes, accounting for staggering by 1/3 panel width.
2. Bend borders: external inwards 10 cm, internal outwards 5 cm.
3. Assemble internal into external wall.
4. Connect adjacent panels with bolts and silicon seals, ensuring staggering by 1/3 panel width between layers.
5. Pressurize interconnected chambers to 0.5 bar.

For wooden panels: Shape wooden core to trapezoid, wrap with PVC sheets on both surfaces, secure edges.
